What Your Restaurant Website Must Have

1. Menu (Easily Updated)

Your menu is the most viewed page. It needs to load fast, look great on phones (where 70%+ of your traffic comes from), and be easy for you to update when prices or items change.

2. Location & Hours

Sounds obvious, but many restaurant sites bury this. Embedded Google Maps, clear hours (including holiday variations), parking info.

3. Reservation System

Integration with OpenTable, Resy, or a built-in form. Diners don't want to call—they want to book from their phones.

4. Photos That Make People Hungry

Professional food photography. Not iPhone shots with bad lighting. This is where restaurants often cheap out and pay for it in lost customers.

5. Online Ordering (If Applicable)

If you offer takeout or delivery, integration with your ordering system. Even better if it's direct (avoiding DoorDash's 30% fees).

6. Private Events Information

If you do private dining, make it easy to inquire. This is high-margin revenue most restaurants leave on the table.

The "I'll Just Use DoorDash" Problem

Many restaurants think their DoorDash/UberEats presence is enough. But those platforms take 15-30% of every order. A website with direct ordering keeps that money in your pocket. Even if only 20% of customers order direct instead of through apps, you're saving thousands monthly.

Why Restaurant Websites Often Disappoint

The PDF Menu Problem

So many restaurant sites just upload a PDF of their paper menu. It's tiny on phones, can't be searched by Google, and looks unprofessional. Your menu should be native HTML—readable, searchable, mobile-friendly.

The "Flash Site" Legacy

If your website was built more than 5 years ago, it might still have design elements from the Flash era. Auto-playing music, decorative animations, hard-to-find navigation. These drive customers away.

The Agency Hostage Situation

Many restaurant marketing agencies lock you into contracts where you pay monthly "maintenance" fees just to update your hours or menu. You should own your website and be able to update it yourself.
